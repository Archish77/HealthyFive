Heart

The heart beats over 100,000 times a day, pumping 5 litres of blood per minute. Cardiovascular disease remains the world's leading cause of death. Early risk detection can prevent fatal events like heart attacks and cardiac arrest.

Your lungs exchange oxygen and CO₂ with every breath — around 22,000 breaths daily. Lung cancer is the leading cancer killer globally. Screening through lifestyle and symptom data enables far earlier detection.

Kidneys filter 200 litres of blood daily, removing waste and regulating pressure. Chronic kidney disease often progresses silently until advanced stages. Biochemical markers caught early can halt progression.

The liver performs over 500 functions — from detoxification to protein synthesis. Liver disease often goes unnoticed until irreversible damage occurs. Enzyme markers and patient data reveal risk before symptoms emerge.
